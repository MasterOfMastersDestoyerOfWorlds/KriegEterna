How To Play


Gameplay

A game of Krieg Eterna is divided into three separate rounds of play, where your objective is to win more rounds than your opponent. Each player's side of the field is split into three rows: melee, ranged, and siege. At the end of each round, the player with the most total strength across all of their rows wins that round.

At the start of the game, each player draws four power cards, nine unit cards, and one King card, then discards down to 11 cards. Unless a card says otherwise, these are the only cards drawn for the entire game. Players take turns playing cards until all players say \'pass\', after which they score the field, discard all cards on the field to the graveyard, and then start the next round.

Cards

There are three separate decks of cards -- the unit deck, power deck, and King deck -- each with a different colored card-backing. Each deck also has its own graveyard, in which discarded cards are placed. Within the decks are different card types, indicated by the symbol in the upper-right corner of a card.

Units

The unit deck consists of Melee, Ranged, and Siege cards. All unit cards have a base strength number in the upper-right corner, indicating how many points they will score for at the end of a round. The total strength of a unit can be increased beyond the base strength using the effects of other cards.

Powers

The power deck consists of Weather, Spy, Jester, and Power cards. These cards are played to the side (not in the melee, ranged, or siege rows), unless otherwise indicated.

Adjacency

If two units with the same base strength are in the same row, they get an adjacency bonusbase strength. which doubles the total strength of both units. You cannot add a third unit to this adjacent pair, but you can have multiple pairs on the field.

Adjacency bonuses only apply to units with three base strength or less. Note that adjacency effects can be enabled or broken by power cards that modify a unit's base strength.

Scoring

To calculate the total strength of each unit, start with the base strength number on the card, add any power cards that affect base strength (all attached powers should apply), then multiply by any adjacency bonus.

To calculate the total strength of each row, sum all of the units in the row, then multiply or divide according to weather and bonuses (always round down if dividing, with a minimum strength of one per unit for the row). After adding all rows together, the player with the highest total strength wins the round.

Caveats

At the end of a round, Kings cannot return to your hand if they were played on the field or sent to the graveyard.

Doubling and halving effects do not remain if the card causing the effect is removed from the field. A King's Weather effects can be cleared without removing the King from the field.

After scoring, all cards on the field are sent to their respective graveyard, unless otherwise noted by a card.

Before playing any cards on your turn, you may pass. Once a player has passed, they may not play any more cards until the next round unless otherwise noted by a card.

Once you have passed, your opponent may play as many cards as they wish before passing themselves.

When drawing cards during a round, you can choose to draw any combination of unit or power cards. King cards cannot be drawn after the game has started.

When asked to reveal a power card, reveal a novel card to your opponent.

When playing with more than two players split into teams, double the number of cards drawn and split them amongst each side.

When playing with more than two players, once a teammate passes they cannot re-enter the round. You do not gain the opportunity to play on their turn.

When playing with more than two players, Power cards should apply once, not once per opponent.

The unit and power graveyards are shared among the players.

The player who dealt or won the last round goes first.

If no player has won two rounds and no player can play any cards, the game is a draw.
